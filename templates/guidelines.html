Injury Guidelines

Essential knowledge for knowing about common injuries

Abrasion

A partial-thickness wound caused by friction against a rough surface, removing the top layers of skin.

Bruise

A contusion occurs when small blood vessels under the skin rupture but the skin remains intact, causing discoloration.

Burn

Tissue damage caused by heat, chemicals, electricity, sunlight, or radiation.

Cut

A wound that breaks through the skin, caused by a sharp object.

Ulcer

An open sore that develops when the skin's surface breaks down and the underlying tissues become exposed.
